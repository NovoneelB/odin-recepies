Banh Mi

As far as Vietnamese street food goes, bánh mì earns top rankings for its irresistibly savory flavors—umami-packed pork, creamy mayonnaise,crunchy vegetables, and a light crispy baguette come together for sandwich perfection.

Bánh mì translates literally to “bread” in Vietnamese and is well-known today as a Vietnamese street food sandwich. The modern day bánh mì sandwich stemmed from French colonialism in Vietnam when French cuisine heavily influenced Vietnamese food culture. After the French defeat, Vietnamese cooks adapted a popular French sandwich made with cold cuts, butter, and cheese into what we recognize today as bánh mì. They incorporated rice flour into the baguette recipe, which produced a lighter interior and paper-thin crust. Mayonnaise replaced butter and veggies and pâté replaced expensive cold cuts.

Ingredients

  • Pate
  • Mayo
  • cucumber
  • green onion
  • red chili
  • pickled carrots
  • Deboned Chicken Thighs
  • lemongrass
  • Vietnamese Baguette or other sub breads of your choice

Steps

  1. Marinade the chicken in Salt, Pickle Juice and LemonGrass overnight.
  2. Bake chicken in oven for 10 min on 425 degrees. Take it out and flip it. Bake again for 8 min on 425 degrees.
  3. Cut bread so that, one side of it is open and the other part of the bread acts as a hinge.
  4. On the inside add mayo and pate
  5. Put the cooked chicken inside the bread
  6. Add the pickled carrots on top
  7. Add Green onin, Cucumber, Other preffered veggies etc. This is the time to customize with your own favorite toppings.
  8. Finally cut one red chilli into small pieces. Add it on the beginnig, middle and end of the sandwitch.

This completes the delicious Banh Mi recepie. Add your own toppings and ingridients to create it your way.
